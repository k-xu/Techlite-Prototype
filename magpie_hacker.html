Magpie hacker caught and jailed for penetrating new Quartz Intel V2 system

Brandon Snow | 21 April, 2050, 4.11PM

9.8k

A hack activist by the name of Ralph Sanchez (known by the handle decinima) was arrested on 20th April 2050 for orchestrating a hack attempt and subsequent breach of the new Quartz Intel V2 neuro-encryption security system from his apartment in Baltimore. While the encryption was not successfully broken, Sanchez has managed to probe a previously undetected crack in the impenetrable fortress of the Quartz company's signature encryptions. He had allegedly achieved this breach through the use of a new wire bypass file (.wbf), a novel crack format that has been making its rounds on the infamous site, the Magpie Collective.

The Magpie Collective is an anonymous hacker activist organization that has gained a reputation in recent years for its assertive stance against the increasing commodification and privatization of information with the rise of information oligopolies like Quartz and Apex. It has several branches ranging from an online forum to a torrent database, where members circulate controversial and newfangled mindware MEM (Mind Encryption Memo) formats devised expressly for the purpose of security breaches and gaining access to protected information from official entities.
